# Trademark & Governance Policy

This document explains what is open and what is protected in the ValiChord project. The short version: **the code is open; the name, the official network, and what "ValiChord-verified" means are protected.** Both halves matter — the openness is what makes ValiChord trustworthy, and the protections are what keep that trust meaningful.

## What is open
ValiChord's source code is released under the **Apache License, Version 2.0**. Under that licence you are free to use, modify, redistribute, and build commercial or non-commercial products on top of the code, including private/proprietary derivatives, provided you comply with the licence terms (including preserving copyright and `NOTICE` attributions).

This openness is deliberate and central to the project: a verification protocol you cannot inspect is just another black box to be trusted. ValiChord's promise — *don't trust us, verify it yourself* — only holds if the code is auditable.

## What is protected
The Apache 2.0 licence covers the **source code only**. It does **not** grant rights to:

- the **"ValiChord" name** or any confusingly similar name;
- the **ValiChord logo** or visual identity;
- representing a fork or derivative as **official, endorsed, or "ValiChord-verified."**

`ValiChord™` is claimed as a trademark of Ceri John. You may make accurate, nominative references (e.g. "compatible with ValiChord," "based on the ValiChord protocol") provided you do not imply that your product is the official ValiChord project or is endorsed by it. You may **not** name a fork "ValiChord," nor present its output as a verification on the official ValiChord network. For any other use of the name or logo, please ask (see Contact).

## The official network and "ValiChord-verified"
A core part of ValiChord's value is the **official network** of nodes and the tamper-evident records it produces. An attestation is only a genuine "ValiChord-verified" record if it was produced through the official protocol and network. Running a private fork does not produce official ValiChord verifications. This distinction protects the meaning of the mark for everyone who relies on it.

## Independence commitment
ValiChord is designed to remain **independent of the parties whose claims it verifies.** A verifier controlled by the party being verified is worthless. Accordingly, the project will not transfer control of the verification protocol or the official network to any party whose claims are, or are likely to be, subject to verification through it. Independence is not a nicety here — it is the source of the trust.

## Governance
ValiChord is currently maintained by **Ceri John** (founder and maintainer). Decisions about the protocol, the canonical/official network, and use of the ValiChord mark rest with the maintainer. As the project and its community grow, this section will be updated to describe a broader governance model. Contributions are welcome under the terms in `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

## Commercial use and licensing
The Apache 2.0 licence already permits commercial use of the code. Separately, the project may offer **commercial licences, certification, hosting, and support** for organisations that need them (for example, the right to use the ValiChord name in a commercial offering, or operation of independent verification on their behalf). These are arranged through direct agreement and do not change the open licence on the code. To discuss commercial use, see Contact.
